Review of the BenQ TK800: a fantastic and bright 4K home projector

In recent years, the price of high-quality projectors has decreased significantly, giving users the opportunity to turn their living rooms into cinemas at an affordable price. But, for a comfortable viewing, most rooms do not have enough dimming. New the BenQ TK800 home 4K projector was created to fix these problems.

The powerful lighting inside modern televisions is better able to cope with ambient light, while many modern projectors can only be used in rooms without Windows or with heavy curtains. However, the TK800 from BenQ is very bright. With a brightness of 3000 lumens, it copes significantly better with ambient light than similar 4K projectors.

With a cost of $ 1500 (99,300 rubles), good color accuracy and the ability to work not only in complete darkness, this projector from BenQ can be taken seriously consider it as a home theater.

The BenQ TK800 boasts the same fantastic image processing processor as the HT2550, which offers 8.3 million pixels and a special HDR mode aimed at providing optimal color balance and contrast's. The main difference between the TK800 and HT2550 is the brightness. Where the HT2550 boasts 2,200 lumens, the TK800 projector offers an impressive 3,000, making it more preferable in lighted rooms.

The 1,2 x optical zoom makes the TK800 easy to install and maintain customization. By placing the projector at a distance of 3.5 m from the screen, I obtained an image measuring approximately 2.5 m diagonally with an aspect ratio of 16: 9. it is Worth noting that the lens here does not have a vertical or horizontal offset, so accurate image correction is limited to three adjustable rubber feet (one in the front and two in the back).

The BenQ home projector is equipped with a number of built-in image modes, including Cinema, Vivid TV and Sport, as well as two customizable modes for more advanced users.

There are no problems installing the TK800, but if your installation involves laying cables, ceiling mounting or other subtle settings, I recommend contacting professionals. Just like the HT2550 model, the design is very compact and lightweight, which makes this equipment very portable.

The gadget does not have an additional lamp in the kit, but the installed one designed for 4,000 hours in full brightness mode and 15,000 hours in SmartEco mode, which adjusts brightness based on the image, to increase the life of the element. This is less than the BenQ HT2550 projector offers, but the TK800 is significantly brighter than its counterpart. In any case, you will get a lot of screen time before you need a replacement lamps.

Design out of the box

The equipment is supplied in a convenient carrying box. The kit includes:

  • BenQ TK800.
  • Power cable.
  • Remote control with backlight.
  • Batteries for console's.

The projector has a rounded shape, like the HT2550 model, but is distinguished by a more beautiful blue front panel. While most people won't buy a 4K projector based on its appearance alone, the design of the BenQ TK800 is as good as it gets perhaps.

Connectors and ports

The BenQ TK800 projector received a large set of audio and video inputs. There are two HDMI ports, one of which is HDCP 2.2 with 4K support, the other for connecting 1080p devices. There is also a VGA input for old PCs, 3.5 mm audio inputs and outputs, and a 12V trigger for connecting nutritions.

The small speaker installed in the TK800 is only suitable for playing basic sounds, including when making office presentations, so I recommend using external speakers. Potential customers are advised to enable their favorite apps streaming devices, Blu-ray players, or game consoles via a good external AV receiver. This will allow you to get the best sound and the ability to select the input signal source. In advance, you only need to make sure that your receiver has support for 4K and HDR, otherwise the BenQ projector will not be able to reveal its full potential potential.

Picture quality

I tested the BenQ TK800 both in total darkness and with the blinds open and the lights on. Generally I am very impressed with his work in all scenarios.

In bright light, the device shows significantly better results results are better than HT2550, especially in Vivid TV mode. I have no hesitation in favoring this mode, since there is no need to search for a dark room or wait for night to watch your favorite movies or TV shows. The only thing I didn't like about Vivid TV is that it tends to flash over bright scenes. This is not very noticeable in the bright it is very noticeable in the room, but it is very noticeable in complete darkness, which is another reason why it is worth sticking to the movie mode, except in places with significant ambient lighting.

However there is a disadvantage to other quality characteristics no effect. This is just the result of using maximum brightness to combat daylight, which allows you to start watching your favorite movie well before sunset.

In a dark room, the TK800 creates stunningly beautiful images, especially when adding HDR. When watching a TV show, I was shocked by the intensity the color scheme, especially the depth of the greens and the sharpness of the Reds, translates all this on a 2.5 m screen in my living room. The 4K resolution looks especially amazing on such a large scale, I really liked how this projector turned my home into a comfortable one mini-cinema.

Benq TK800 warranty and alternatives

BenQ offers a three-year warranty on the parts and operation of the equipment, with the exception of lamps. Lamps on new devices have a warranty of 180 days or 500 hours from the date of purchase.

As alternatively, you can consider the BenQ HT2550, which has the same color accuracy. I also recommend looking at the Optoma UHD50, which boasts the same brightness of 3,000 lumens, but costs $ 100 (6,600 rubles.) cheaper.

Result

The new BenQ TK800 projector is a breakthrough in the emerging segment of 4K home cinema, it is excellent and extremely versatile and ranks among the best of all our tested solutions. For the user choosing their first projector, there is everything you need, no frills.

Is it worth buying?

Yes. If you looking for a home theater projector that has good performance and brightness when illuminated, the TK800 is one of the best here.

Advantages of the BenQ TK800

  • 3000 lumens brightness.
  • Excellent color accuracy.
  • High HDR performance.

Disadvantages of the TK800

  • Visible overexposure in the maximum brightness.
